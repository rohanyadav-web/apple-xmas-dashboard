03

How does Christmas budget affect quantity sold?

Understanding the relationship between declared budget and actual purchasing behaviour helps product managers set SKU depth and decide where to concentrate premium vs. entry-level placement. This was entirely missing from the original VOTD.

Units sold by budget tier

All three tiers sell a near-identical number of units — volume is not driven by budget size.

High: 57,046 units. Medium: 57,498 units. Low: 56,175 units.
Key finding: Discounting to drive volume won't work — all budgets already move similar unit counts. Focus on upselling within existing buyers.

Revenue by budget tier

High-budget customers generate slightly more revenue — they favour pricier SKUs, not higher quantities.

High: $9.57M. Medium: $9.59M. Low: $9.37M.
SKU strategy: High-budget shoppers prefer premium lines. Ensure MacBook, iPhone Pro, and Watch Ultra variants are prominently stocked and merchandised for this segment.

Purchase channels & payment methods

Age group 1–11 excluded — not a meaningful independent purchasing demographic for Apple products. Analysis focuses on teens (12–17) and adults (18+) where actual buying decisions are made.

Where customers shop Christmas gifts

In-store dominates both age groups. Online and Xmas Market are almost identical — neither has a meaningful channel advantage.

Age 12–17
Age 18+
In-store leads both age groups. Online and Xmas Market are near-equal and much smaller.
Channel priority: In-store remains the dominant purchase environment for both age groups. Investment in store experience and product placement clearly outweighs online optimisation for Christmas gifting.

Most common payment methods

Credit card leads strongly, followed by PayPal. Cash is weakest — confirming a digital-first audience across both age groups.

Age 12–17
Age 18+
Credit card dominates, followed by PayPal. Cash is lowest across both age groups.
Checkout design: Prioritise credit card and PayPal acceptance, including Apple Pay integration, in both online and in-store environments to reduce friction.
